                    Advancing Tokenization 
at Institutional Scale
                

                DTCC is leading the evolution of institutional finance by securely tokenizing real-world assets (RWAs). Our solutions combine the resilience of traditional markets with the innovation of blockchain technology—enhancing capital efficiency, reducing operational costs, and unlocking new financial services.

How to Engage
Evolving Capital Markets 
with Secure Tokenization

The Depository Trust Company (DTC), a subsidiary of The Depository Trust & Clearing Corporation (DTCC), has been authorized to offer a new and innovative tokenization service for select DTC-custodied assets after receiving a No-Action Letter from the SEC on December 11, 2025.

This important federal regulatory milestone paves the way for DTC Participants and their clients to begin to tokenize select stocks, ETFs and fixed-income securities within a secure and regulated environment – a critical first step to bringing the U.S. capital markets on-chain, with 24/7 access and connectivity to the expanding blockchain ecosystem. 


We are excited to collaborate with the industry and leverage our 50+ years of expertise transforming global markets to establish a resilient, trusted and interoperable digital asset ecosystem, developed in partnership with key stakeholders and the industry.


DTC’s new tokenization service is anticipated to be production-ready in the second half of 2026.

A Unique Moment in Time
After years of industry experimentation and progress with distributed ledger technology (DLT), DTCC is driving digital asset acceptance and adoption in partnership with the industry.
Our new tokenization service will serve as the bridge between TradFi
and DeFi and, importantly, offer the same investor protections and
ownership rights as traditional book-entry securities currently held in
custody by DTC. 

Upon launch next year, DTC Participants and their clients will be able to convert select
supported assets between traditional book-entry and digital tokenized
forms, which will create secure and compliant access to an emerging
web3 ecosystem. 
The service will initially support highly liquid assets, including the
Russell 1000, ETFs tracking major indices, and U.S. Treasury bills,
bonds and notes. DTCCs open tokenization model will facilitate the
use of multiple token standards, embedded compliance and multi-blockchain interoperability. 
A Turning Point in Market Infrastructure

Tokenization extends trusted market infrastructure into the digital asset ecosystem by representing real-world assets on blockchain within a controlled and regulated environment. Built on existing market infrastructure, the DTC Tokenization Service is designed to support established ownership rights and investor protections while enabling standards-based, interoperable access to blockchain networks.

Programmable Assets
When assets are tokenized by DTC, firms will have access to both blockchain-based and traditional liquidity pools. Since both the traditional form and tokenized version share the same CUSIP, assets can freely move between traditional and blockchain ecosystems to provide immediate access to liquidity pools across the global ecosystem. In addition, tokenized assets will seamlessly flow between registered Participants’ wallets within preapproved blockchain ecosystems, 24/7.
Exactly like traditional securities, DTC-issued tokens will carry all the same legal and economic rights and investor protections – as well as deliver certain benefits of corporate action processing regardless of whether participants hold assets in either token form or traditional form, or both.
DTC-issued tokens will incorporate comprehensive controls, such as the ability to Mint, Burn, Force Transfer, Clawback, Pause/Unpause, and Freeze/Unfreeze, to help meet compliance needs, including distribution and recovery capabilities.


To further support control, DTCC’s ComposerX LedgerScan will track ownership in near real time and enable the consolidation and harmonization of data from various traditional and blockchain ledgers to create a consolidated view.
DTCC’s smart contracts will initially incorporate compliance and distribution controls and connect tokenized assets with the asset reference data that is stored on-chain. We plan to enable automation throughout the entire trade lifecycle, ranging from issuance to corporate actions, in subsequent releases to meet industry needs. 
How will assets be converted between traditional and tokenized form?
When a Participant wants to hold their assets in tokenized form, they will issue a “conversion order” instructing DTC to convert the assets to token form and deliver them to their registered digital wallet. When a Participant wants to convert the token back to traditional form in its traditional DTC account, it can simply instruct DTC to make that conversion.
